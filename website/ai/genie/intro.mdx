> Not everyone wants to *build* an agent. Sometimes you just want to **ask your data a
> question in plain English and get a trustworthy, governed answer.** That's Genie
> and AI/BI — and it's a topic of its own.

## What this topic will cover

Where the **Databricks AI** track is about building agents and GenAI apps, this track
is about **conversational analytics** — letting business users (and agents) query
governed data in natural language, and building the BI layer around it.

Planned lessons:

- **What is Genie?** Natural-language questions over Unity Catalog tables — Genie writes and runs the SQL for you.
- **Setting up a Genie space** — choosing tables, and why good table modeling, column comments, and sample queries drive accuracy.
- **Making Genie accurate** — instructions, curated examples, evaluation of natural-language-to-SQL, and handling ambiguity.
- **AI/BI dashboards & Genie in the flow of work** — embedding conversational analytics where users already are.
- **Governance & trust** — Unity Catalog permissions so users only see data they're allowed to; auditing NL-to-SQL.
- **Genie as an agent tool** — plugging a Genie space into an agent or a Multi-agent Supervisor as the structured-data tool.
- **When to use Genie vs a custom agent vs a dashboard.**
