# Platform Security & Compliance

> The unglamorous plumbing that lets security teams say yes to the agentic stack — identity auto-sync, zero-trust ingress, private networking, and a pile of new compliance badges. Read the GA/Preview labels carefully; a lot of this isn't shippable yet.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/whats-new-databricks-platform-security-and-compliance-data-ai-summit-2026).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="Mixed" /> — AIM (Entra ID) GA; Okta & Context-Based Ingress Public Preview; Private Network Gateway Private Preview.

## What shipped
- One theme — **scale AI securely** — across four pillars: identity, ingress, networking, compliance.
- **Automatic Identity Management (AIM) for Entra ID** is **GA on AWS and GCP** (already GA on Azure). **AIM for Okta** is **Public Preview** on AWS and GCP. Auto-syncs users and groups so you stop babysitting SCIM.
- **Context-Based Ingress (CBI)** — **Public Preview** on all three clouds — enforces zero-trust access by network source, identity, and scope for Genie, dashboards, Databricks Apps, and AI Gateway endpoints.
- **Private Network Gateway** — **Private Preview, Azure only** — secures outbound connections to private data sources, APIs, and enterprise apps.
- **Private Link for Lakebase, Zerobus, and services** is **GA on AWS**, **Public Preview on Azure**; **Inbound Private Link for account-level resources** (Genie, account console) is **Beta** (end of June 2026).
- Compliance: **HITRUST** GA on all three clouds, **ISMAP** (Japan) GA on AWS and Azure, **AWS GovCloud** expanded with new services (GA), and **Azure serverless compliance** GA. Still on the roadmap: **Saudi frameworks (CCC, DCC, ECC)** on GCP, **AWS serverless compliance**, and **FedRAMP High on Azure Commercial** — "later this summer."

## Where it fits
- This is the **governance and security layer** — the plumbing beneath Unity Catalog and everything agentic sitting above it.
- **Identity teams** get the biggest immediate win: AIM kills manual provisioning drift from Entra ID or Okta.
- **Regulated shops** — healthcare (HITRUST), public sector (GovCloud/FedRAMP), JP/SA markets — pick up new procurement checkboxes without a services engagement.
- **Network architects** keep traffic off the public internet: Private Network Gateway plus expanded Private Link now cover Lakebase and Zerobus, not just the classic surfaces.
- **Zero-trust shops** already running conditional access in Entra/Okta can extend those policies to Databricks ingress via CBI.
- Chains cleanly with **Unity Catalog** and **Unity AI Gateway** for identity-to-model governance.

## Where it falls short
- **Preview is doing the heavy lifting.** AIM for Okta, Context-Based Ingress, and Private Network Gateway are all **not GA** — architect around them cautiously, if at all, in prod.
- **Cloud-by-cloud parity is uneven.** AIM for Entra ID: GA on AWS/GCP/Azure. Private Network Gateway: Azure only. Private Link for Lakebase/Zerobus: GA on AWS, Preview on Azure. What you can deploy depends heavily on which cloud you're on.
- **Their compliance coverage is not your certification.** Databricks holding HITRUST/ISMAP/FedRAMP authorization does not make *your* workload compliant. Shared-responsibility scoping — and the audit — is still on you.
- **Zero-trust is a self-inflicted-outage risk.** CBI can silently lock out CI jobs, BI tools, and partners if a policy is misjudged. Budget for a tuning phase in non-prod before you point it at anything real.
- **Dated items are roadmap, not GA.** FedRAMP High on Azure and AWS serverless compliance are "later this summer" targets. Don't put them in a plan that ships before they do.

## My take
The least flashy page from DAIS 2026 and probably one of the most genuinely useful. None of this is a headline, but it's the work that lets a security team sign off on the agentic stack everyone else is excited about.

Split it by what's actually shippable today. **AIM for Entra ID — adopt now.** It's GA on all three clouds, low-risk, and it removes real provisioning toil; there's no reason to wait. **The GA Private Link, GovCloud, HITRUST, and ISMAP pieces — adopt or pilot** if you're already regulated: real procurement value, but validate the scope against your auditors, not the blog post. **Context-Based Ingress and Private Network Gateway — pilot in a non-prod workspace first.** Zero-trust misconfiguration is the top way to hurt yourself here. **AIM for Okta and FedRAMP High on Azure — watch**; revisit when they're GA and authorized respectively.

Market context matters: identity auto-sync and Private Link parity are table stakes against Snowflake and the hyperscalers, not differentiation. This is Databricks closing gaps, not opening a lead — and the compliance breadth, impressive as it reads, is inheritance, not absolution. The certification burden on you barely moves.

**Bottom line:** Adopt the GA identity and networking pieces now, pilot zero-trust carefully, and treat everything labeled Preview or "later this summer" as a roadmap, not a plan.
