# Platform Security & Compliance

> A security push aimed at scaling AI safely: automatic identity, zero-trust ingress, private networking, and broad new compliance coverage — but read the Preview vs GA labels carefully.

*Announced at DAIS 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: community deck — verify officially.*

## Overview
- The deck frames a single theme: **scale AI securely** via four pillars — identity, ingress, networking, compliance.
- **Automatic Identity Management (AIM) for Entra ID** is **GA on AWS and GCP**; **AIM for Okta** is in **Preview** (per the deck).
- **Context-Based Ingress** — a **zero-trust** access model gating who can reach the workspace and from where.
- **Private Network Gateway** is in **Preview**; **Private Link expanded** to cover **Lakebase, Zerobus, and account-level resources**.
- Compliance additions (as presented): **HITRUST across all clouds**, **ISMAP** (Japan), **Saudi frameworks**, and **expanded GovCloud**.
- **FedRAMP High on Azure** is **coming (summer 2026)** — i.e. not yet available at announcement.

## Where it fits
- **Governance and security layer** of the stack — the plumbing under Unity Catalog and the agentic tools above it.
- **Identity teams**: AIM auto-syncs users/groups from Entra ID or Okta, cutting manual SCIM/provisioning drift.
- **Regulated industries** (healthcare via HITRUST, public sector via GovCloud/FedRAMP, JP/SA markets) get new procurement checkboxes.
- **Network/security architects**: Private Network Gateway + expanded Private Link keep traffic — including to Lakebase and Zerobus — off the public internet.
- **Zero-trust shops**: Context-Based Ingress pairs with conditional-access policies already run in Entra/Okta.
- Pairs naturally with **Unity Catalog** and **Unity AI Gateway** for an identity-to-model governance chain.

## Where it fails
- **Preview is doing heavy lifting.** AIM for Okta, Private Network Gateway, and (arguably) FedRAMP High are **not GA** — do not architect around them yet.
- **Cloud-by-cloud unevenness**: AIM for Entra ID is GA on **AWS/GCP** but the deck is quiet on Azure GA timing — awkward given Entra is Microsoft's own directory.
- **Compliance coverage != your certification.** Databricks holding HITRUST/ISMAP/FedRAMP authorization does not make *your* workload compliant — shared-responsibility scoping is still on you.
- **Zero-trust rollout is complex**: Context-Based Ingress can silently lock out CI, BI tools, and partners if policies are misjudged; expect a painful tuning phase.
- **Private Link expansion adds cost and config sprawl** — more endpoints, DNS, and per-service wiring to maintain across accounts.
- **Forward-looking dates slip.** "Summer 2026" FedRAMP High is a promise, not a fact; treat all deck names/labels as unverified.

## Response
- **Verdict for AIM (Entra ID, GA clouds): Adopt now** — auto-sync is low-risk, high-toil-reduction, and it's actually GA.
- **Private Link expansion & GovCloud/HITRUST: Adopt / Pilot** if you're already regulated — real procurement value, but validate scope against auditors.
- **Context-Based Ingress & Private Network Gateway: Pilot** in a non-prod workspace first — zero-trust misconfig is the top self-inflicted outage risk here.
- **AIM for Okta & FedRAMP High on Azure: Watch** — Preview/forward-looking; revisit when GA and authorized.
- Market context: identity auto-sync and Private Link parity are **table stakes** vs Snowflake and the hyperscalers, not differentiation — this is Databricks closing gaps, not opening a lead.

## Personal impression
- The least flashy DAIS page and probably the most *useful* — this is the unglamorous work that lets security teams say yes to the agentic stack above it.
- But the announcement leans on **Preview** and **"coming"** labels; strip those out and the genuinely-shippable news is AIM for Entra ID plus some Private Link endpoints.
- Compliance breadth reads impressive, yet it's inheritance, not absolution — the certification burden on customers barely moves.
- **Hype level: Low.** **Bottom line:** Adopt the GA identity + networking pieces now, pilot zero-trust carefully, and treat everything marked Preview or "summer 2026" as a roadmap, not a plan.
