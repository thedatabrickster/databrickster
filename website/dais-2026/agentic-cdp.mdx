# Agentic CDP (CustomerLake)

> A pitched "new species of CDP" — built agent-first, with a Golden Context (not just a Golden Record) driving always-on, real-time "Infinity Campaigns" from inside the lakehouse.

*Announced at DAIS 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: community deck — verify officially.*

## Overview

- Framed as an **agent-first Customer Data Platform** — the deck's names **"CustomerLake"** and **"Infinity Campaigns"** read as branding and are likely deck-coined or a partner concept, not a confirmed Databricks product.
- Core idea: a **Golden Context**, not just a Golden Record — capturing **intent, business objectives, and the rationale behind past decisions**, beyond static demographics.
- **Always-on, real-time engagement loops** replace episodic batch campaigns; the pitch is true 1:1 personalization via LLMs and agents.
- Positioned to **live inside the lakehouse**, so agent actions inherit **Unity Catalog entitlements** while humans set goals and guardrails.
- Deck metrics (unverified, as presented): **segment-of-one** personalization, **millisecond** responsiveness vs. batch, **continuous** loops vs. episodic batches, and **the same governance** as human operators.

## Where it fits

- **Activation / marketing layer** sitting on top of the lakehouse — pairs a customer 360 with agents that decide and act. See [the overview](/dais-2026).
- For teams already consolidating customer data in **Unity Catalog** who want activation without shipping it all to a separate SaaS CDP.
- **Governance-in-place** is the sharpest angle: if agent outreach inherits UC entitlements and lineage, compliance review gets easier than in a bolt-on tool.
- **Golden Context** is a genuinely useful reframe — carrying *why* a decision was made helps agents avoid repeating rejected offers or ignoring stated intent.
- Complements **Genie/agent tooling** for the reasoning layer and real-time serving (e.g. Lakebase-style low-latency stores) for the responsiveness claim.
- Best suited to **high-volume B2C** (retail, media, telco) where 1:1 personalization at scale actually moves revenue.

## Where it fails

- **Names are almost certainly branding.** "CustomerLake" and "Infinity Campaigns" should be treated as deck-coined / partner marketing until an official Databricks page confirms otherwise.
- **CDP is a crowded, mature category** — Salesforce Data Cloud, Adobe Real-Time CDP, and Segment/Twilio all have years of activation, identity resolution, and connector ecosystems this would need to match.
- **Agent-driven outreach is a brand and compliance risk** — autonomous 1:1 messaging invites tone errors, hallucinated offers, and CAN-SPAM / GDPR / TCPA exposure; "humans set goals" doesn't remove liability for what agents send.
- **All headline metrics are unverified** — "millisecond" responsiveness, "segment-of-one," and "same governance as human ops" are deck claims with no benchmark, latency budget, or reference customer.
- **"Golden Context" is hard to build** — inferring intent and decision rationale reliably is an unsolved data problem; garbage context yields confidently wrong personalization.
- **Lock-in** — an activation layer welded to Unity Catalog is sticky; the batch-vs-realtime and lakehouse-native framing understates connector and identity-graph gaps.
- **Maturity/status unknown** — likely conceptual or early-preview; treat "always-on" as aspirational, not shipping.

## Response

- **Verdict: Watch.** Compelling architecture story, but confirm what (if anything) is a real product before planning around it — the names smell like branding.
- **Pilot only if** you already run Databricks as your customer 360 and want to test governed activation on **one low-risk, opt-in channel** with human approval in the loop.
- **Ignore** if you have a working Salesforce/Adobe/Segment CDP — rip-and-replace for an unproven concept isn't justified.
- **Adopt now: no** — no confirmed product, no benchmarks, and real compliance exposure in autonomous outreach.
- **Market context** — the "governed activation inside the warehouse" trend is real (see reverse-ETL and Snowflake/Databricks-native CDPs like Hightouch/Census), so the *category* is plausible even if this specific offering isn't confirmed.

## Personal impression

- **Golden Context** is the one idea I'd steal — carrying intent and decision rationale is a real gap in every CDP I've seen, and lakehouse-native governance is a legitimate edge.
- But "Infinity Campaigns" and always-on agentic outreach is exactly where hype outruns safety; the compliance and brand blast radius of a hallucinating agent hitting real customers is severe.
- **Hype level: High.** Great framing, buzzy names, zero verified metrics, and a category full of entrenched incumbents.
- **Bottom line:** Watch and confirm it's a real product; borrow the Golden Context concept now, but keep any agentic outreach human-approved and single-channel until governance and accuracy are proven.
