# Agentic CDP (CustomerLake)

> Databricks' "new species of CDP" — built agent-first, with a **Golden Context** (not just a Golden Record) driving always-on, real-time **Infinity Campaigns** from inside the lakehouse.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/what-is-an-agentic-cdp).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="Not stated" /> — the announcement did not give a release stage.

## Overview

- Databricks introduces the **Agentic CDP** and its product, **[CustomerLake](https://www.databricks.com/blog/what-is-an-agentic-cdp)** — described as "a native Agentic CDP for the Databricks platform" (authors: Tasso Argyros, Ali Ghodsi, Reynold Xin).
- Core reframe: a **Golden Context**, not just a **Golden Record**. The Golden Record answers *who the customer is*; Golden Context adds *what the business is trying to accomplish right now* and *what's already been tried with this customer and how they responded*.
- **Infinity Campaigns** — the headline capability: "autonomous, continuously compounding engagement loops" that adapt to new context signals to reshape message, timing, and channel on the fly, for always-on 1:1 personalization.
- **Embedded in the data foundation**, not alongside it — the CDP lives inside the data platform with native integration to governance like **Unity Catalog**.
- **Born agentic** — agents are first-class operators and agentic operation is the primary mode from the start, not a bolted-on feature.
- Databricks' stated rationale: agentic buying lifecycles move in **milliseconds** while legacy CDPs run **batch cycles of days/weeks**, and agents need hyper-personalization down to **segments of one**.

## Where it fits

- **Activation / marketing layer** on top of the lakehouse — a customer 360 paired with agents that decide and act. See [the overview](/dais-2026).
- For teams already consolidating customer data in **Unity Catalog** who want activation without shipping it all to a separate SaaS CDP.
- **Governance-in-place** is the sharpest angle: agent outreach that inherits UC entitlements and lineage makes compliance review easier than in a bolt-on tool.
- **Golden Context** is a genuinely useful reframe — carrying *why* a decision was made helps agents avoid repeating rejected offers or ignoring stated intent.
- Complements **Genie / agent tooling** for the reasoning layer and real-time serving for the responsiveness claim.
- Best suited to **high-volume B2C** (retail, media, telco) where 1:1 personalization at scale actually moves revenue.

## Where it fails

- **CDP is a crowded, mature category** — Salesforce Data Cloud, Adobe Real-Time CDP, and Segment/Twilio all bring years of activation, identity resolution, and connector ecosystems this must match.
- **Agent-driven outreach is a brand and compliance risk** — autonomous 1:1 messaging invites tone errors, hallucinated offers, and CAN-SPAM / GDPR / TCPA exposure; "agents as first-class operators" doesn't remove liability for what they send.
- **Headline claims lack benchmarks** — "milliseconds," "segments of one," and always-on loops are stated as design goals in the launch post, without a published latency budget or reference customer.
- **"Golden Context" is hard to build** — inferring intent and decision rationale reliably is an unsolved data problem; garbage context yields confidently wrong personalization.
- **Lock-in** — an activation layer welded to Unity Catalog is sticky; the batch-vs-realtime, lakehouse-native framing can understate connector and identity-graph gaps.
- **Availability not stated** — the post doesn't specify GA / Preview / Beta status, so treat "always-on" as the product vision until a rollout/status page confirms timing.

## Response

- **Verdict: Watch closely.** This is a real Databricks product with a compelling architecture story; the open question is maturity and rollout timing, not legitimacy.
- **Pilot if** you already run Databricks as your customer 360 and want to test governed activation on **one low-risk, opt-in channel** with human approval in the loop.
- **Hold** if you have a working Salesforce/Adobe/Segment CDP — rip-and-replace before benchmarks and availability are clear isn't justified.
- **Adopt now: cautiously** — confirm status and connector coverage, and keep autonomous outreach gated given the compliance exposure.
- **Market context** — "governed activation inside the warehouse" is a real trend (reverse-ETL and lakehouse-native CDPs like Hightouch/Census), so the category is well-founded, not speculative.

## Personal impression

- **Golden Context** is the one idea I'd steal — carrying intent and decision rationale is a real gap in every CDP I've seen, and lakehouse-native governance is a legitimate edge.
- **Infinity Campaigns** and always-on agentic outreach is exactly where hype can outrun safety; a hallucinating agent hitting real customers has a severe compliance and brand blast radius.
- **Hype level: High.** Strong framing and buzzy naming, but no published metrics yet and a category full of entrenched incumbents.
- **Bottom line:** A genuine, well-architected Databricks entry — borrow the Golden Context concept now, but keep any agentic outreach human-approved and single-channel until governance, accuracy, and availability are proven.
