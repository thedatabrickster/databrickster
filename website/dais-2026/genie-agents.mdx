# Genie Agents

> Ask data questions in plain language; get governed, visualized answers over the lakehouse — no SQL, no BI tool.

*Announced at DAIS 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: community deck — verify officially.*

## Overview

- **Conversational analytics agents** — ask data questions in natural language and get trusted, governed answers with no SQL or BI tool needed, per the deck.
- **Agentic reasoning** turns a question into SQL over the lakehouse, returns visualized results, and suggests follow-up questions.
- **Expert instructions** plus predefined **trusted answers** (verified SQL) keep domain-specific answers accurate.
- **Adapts to org change** and learns from thumbs up/down feedback; **activity monitoring** and **accuracy benchmarking** are built in (deck-attributed, unverified).
- **Delivery options** — standalone Genie spaces, embedded in dashboards, or via API into Teams, Slack, and Glean.

## Where it fits

- **Semantic / consumption layer** of the Modern Databricks Stack — self-serve analytics on top of Unity Catalog-governed data. See [the overview](/dais-2026).
- Best for **business users and analysts** who need ad-hoc answers without waiting on a BI backlog or writing SQL.
- Pairs with **Unity Catalog** (governed tables, lineage, permissions) so generated SQL inherits row/column access controls.
- Fits orgs that already curate **certified metrics and semantic definitions** — trusted answers turn those into reusable, verified responses.
- **Embeddable** — surface the same agent inside existing dashboards or chat tools (Teams, Slack, Glean) rather than forcing a new destination.
- Complements, not replaces, dashboards: good for the long tail of one-off questions that would otherwise become ad-hoc tickets.

## Where it fails

- **Text-to-SQL accuracy degrades on messy schemas** — cryptic column names, undocumented joins, and duplicated tables produce confidently wrong SQL. The accuracy floor is your metadata quality, not the model.
- **Reliability depends on curation** — without expert instructions and trusted answers, results are inconsistent; the "no SQL needed" promise quietly assumes someone did heavy semantic modeling first.
- **Governance of generated SQL** — a plausible-looking query can silently misaggregate or double-count. Answers need a verification path, and non-technical users can't spot the error.
- **Metrics unverified** — "activity monitoring" and "accuracy benchmarking" are deck-attributed with no published numbers; treat benchmark claims as marketing until Databricks publishes methodology.
- **Naming overlap** — "Genie Agents" collides with **Genie One** and the broader Genie brand; expect confusion about what is a space, an agent, and an assistant.
- **Preview maturity** — feedback learning and org-change adaptation are hard to verify; assume preview-grade behavior and drift over time.
- **Lock-in** — trusted answers and expert instructions are Databricks-specific curation that doesn't port to another BI stack.

## Response

- **Verdict: Pilot.** The self-serve value is real, but only after you invest in semantic curation — pilot on one well-modeled domain, not the whole warehouse.
- **Adopt now only if** you already have certified metrics and clean, documented tables in Unity Catalog; then trusted answers pay off quickly.
- **Watch** the accuracy-benchmarking and feedback-learning claims until official docs confirm methodology and numbers.
- **Don't** point it at raw, undocumented schemas and expect trustworthy answers — you will get confident nonsense.
- **Market context** — competes with **Snowflake Cortex Analyst**, **ThoughtSpot**, and **Power BI Q&A**. Databricks' edge is native Unity Catalog governance and lineage on the generated SQL, not the text-to-SQL itself.

## Personal impression

- The honest version of this product is "a natural-language front end for a well-curated semantic layer" — the agent is only as good as the trusted answers and metadata behind it.
- Text-to-SQL demos beautifully and disappoints on real enterprise schemas; the gap between the two is exactly the curation work the pitch downplays.
- **Hype level: Medium-High.** Conversational analytics is the most over-promised category in the deck, and every major vendor ships a near-identical feature.
- **Bottom line:** Pilot on one curated domain, lean hard on trusted answers, and keep a human who can read SQL in the loop before anyone makes decisions off Genie's output.
