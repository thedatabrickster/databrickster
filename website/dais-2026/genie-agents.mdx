# Genie Agents

> Turn a prompt into a shareable, autonomous agent that reasons over your data and acts across business tools.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/introducing-genie-one-genie-ontology-and-genie-agents).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="Not stated" /> — the announcement did not give a release stage.

## Overview

- **Genie Spaces is evolving into Genie Agents** — curated, domain-specific AI agents built on the more than one million Genie Spaces customers have already created.
- **Take autonomous action** — Genie Agents use the same tools as Genie One: MCP connections, scheduled tasks, document and artifact generation, and writes to external systems, to complete multi-step workflows without oversight.
- **Reason over unstructured data, not just tables and views** — agents can be grounded in documents, files, and knowledge sources alongside structured data.
- **Spin up from a single prompt** in Genie One or Genie Code, then scope it, benchmark it, and share it for teammates to use or customize.
- **Built on Genie lineage** — Genie Spaces are curated, governed chat experiences scoped to specific topics, with verified logic and benchmarks; Genie Agents carry that curation forward.

## Where it fits

- **Semantic / consumption layer** of the Modern Databricks Stack — self-serve analytics and action on top of Unity Catalog-governed data. See [the overview](/dais-2026).
- Lets **domain experts scale their expertise** by turning trusted rules, data, and workflows into agents the whole team can rely on.
- Pairs with **Unity Catalog** (governed tables, lineage, permissions) so agent actions inherit access controls.
- Sits alongside **Genie One** (the data-smart AI coworker delivered across Slack, Teams, and mobile) and **Genie Ontology** (the automatic context graph over tables, queries, dashboards, and pipelines).
- Extends beyond Q&A into **action** — MCP tool use, scheduled tasks, and writes to external systems turn answers into completed workflows.

## Where it fails

- **Autonomy raises the stakes** — agents that write to external systems and act "without need for oversight" can propagate a wrong assumption into real changes, not just a wrong chart.
- **Reliability depends on curation** — benchmarks and scoping are only as good as the trusted rules and data behind them; a thinly scoped agent inherits the messiness of the underlying schema.
- **Unstructured grounding is hard to verify** — reasoning over documents and files broadens the surface for confidently wrong answers, and non-technical users can't easily spot the error.
- **Naming overlap** — Genie Agents, Genie Spaces, Genie One, Genie Code, and Genie Ontology are a crowded brand; expect confusion about what is a space, an agent, and a coworker.
- **Lock-in** — trusted rules, benchmarks, and MCP wiring are Databricks-specific curation that doesn't port to another stack.

## Response

- **Verdict: Pilot.** The action-taking value is real, but only after you invest in scoping and benchmarking — pilot on one well-modeled domain, not the whole warehouse.
- **Adopt now only if** you already have curated, benchmarked Genie Spaces and clean, governed tables in Unity Catalog; the migration path is then natural.
- **Constrain autonomy early** — gate external-system writes and scheduled actions behind review until an agent's benchmarks earn trust.
- **Don't** point an agent at raw, undocumented data or knowledge sources and expect trustworthy action.
- **Market context** — competes with **Snowflake Cortex**, **ThoughtSpot**, and **Power BI Q&A**. Databricks' edge is native Unity Catalog governance plus action across tools, not text-to-SQL alone.

## Personal impression

- The real story here is the shift from "answer questions" to "take action" — Genie Agents are Genie Spaces with hands, and that is both the value and the risk.
- The single-prompt creation flow will demo beautifully; the durable question is whether scoping and benchmarking hold up on real enterprise data and documents.
- **Hype level: Medium-High.** Autonomous business-tool agents are the most over-promised category of the summit, and every major vendor is racing here.
- **Bottom line:** Pilot on one curated, benchmarked domain, keep external writes on a short leash, and keep a human who understands the data in the loop before agents act on decisions.
