# OpenSharing & SecureConnect

> Delta Sharing grows up: share not just data but models, agents, and governed Genie Agents across clouds and vendors — and a Databricks-managed proxy kills the per-recipient firewall dance.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Sources: [Databricks blog — OpenSharing](https://www.databricks.com/blog/introducing-opensharing-next-evolution-delta-sharing-agentic-era) · [Databricks blog — SecureConnect](https://www.databricks.com/blog/introducing-opensharing-secureconnect).*

## Overview
- The **next evolution of Delta Sharing** — share data (Delta, Iceberg, Parquet), AI models, autonomous agents, and **Genie Agents** across any cloud, vendor, and on-prem, governed by Unity Catalog.
- **No data copying** — recipients query live data directly at the source, with end-to-end governance and audit logging in Unity Catalog.
- Open posture: the protocol is now **hosted by the Linux Foundation** at opensharing.io, with an open spec plus Databricks' enterprise implementation.
- **Iceberg REST Catalog API** support, so any Iceberg-compatible client can read shares; providers can also share from external catalogs (AWS Glue, Hive Metastore, Snowflake Horizon).
- On-prem storage partners: **MinIO (GA)**; **Everpure (Private Preview)**, **Qumulo** and **VAST Data** coming soon; **Cohesity, Commvault, NetApp, Nutanix** by year-end — together managing hundreds of exabytes of enterprise data.
- **SecureConnect (Public Preview)** — a Databricks-managed proxy that routes storage access for recipients, ending per-recipient IP allowlisting: providers allowlist Databricks endpoints once.
- SecureConnect specifics: **zero config for serverless recipients**, optional NCC private link to provider storage, **mutual TLS**, and **data stays in the provider's storage bucket** (recipients never touch the bucket directly). Works across regions and clouds, with customer-managed or Databricks Default Storage.
- Delta Sharing today: **28,000+ recipients**, and **33% of shares are cross-platform**.

## Where it fits
- **Data collaboration layer** — the outbound edge of the lakehouse for partners, subsidiaries, and customers.
- Teams sharing across **multiple clouds or vendors** who currently hand-maintain firewall allowlists for every recipient.
- **Productizing analytics**: ship a governed Genie Agent to a customer instead of raw tables.
- **Model and agent distribution** — internal platform teams handing curated models/agents to downstream business units.
- Pairs with **Unity Catalog** (single governance plane) and **serverless recipients** that get the zero-config SecureConnect path.
- Fits **on-prem / hybrid** shops via the MinIO/VAST/Qumulo partner story and Iceberg REST Catalog interop.
- SecureConnect suits orgs where **security review of firewall changes** is the real bottleneck to any data share.

## Where it fails
- **Sharing agents across vendors raises governance and liability questions** — who owns a bad answer, whose logs, whose prompt policy wins? Largely unaddressed.
- **The managed proxy is a new dependency and trust boundary** — SecureConnect becomes a single control point and potential single point of failure for all external sharing.
- **"Open" is in tension with a Databricks-managed control plane** — the spec is Linux Foundation, but SecureConnect is Databricks-operated infrastructure; recipients still lean on it.
- **Zero-config only for serverless recipients** — everyone else still touches NCC/private link, so the "configure once" promise is partial.
- **Preview and partner maturity risk** — SecureConnect is Public Preview; most on-prem partners are Private Preview or "coming soon", and cross-vendor agent sharing is novel, so expect early gaps in RBAC, audit, and revocation semantics.
- **Egress economics are unstated** — the posts don't quantify egress or storage cost implications of live cross-cloud reads; model your own.

## Response
- **Verdict: Pilot** SecureConnect + data/model sharing — the network-config elimination is a concrete, real pain removed with low blast radius.
- **Watch** cross-vendor Genie Agent sharing — genuinely new, but wait for clear liability, audit, and revocation answers before exposing agents externally.
- Treat SecureConnect as **Public Preview** — validate failure modes and support posture before it sits in the path of production shares.
- Market context: this competes with **Snowflake Data Sharing / cloud-native shares (AWS Data Exchange, BigQuery Analytics Hub)**; the differentiator is agent/Genie sharing plus managed networking, not raw data sharing.
- Keep a **portable fallback** — the Linux Foundation OpenSharing spec is the open part; lean on it so you are not fully bound to the managed proxy.

## Personal impression
- SecureConnect is the quietly useful piece — killing per-recipient firewall coordination solves a boring, real problem that stalls most external sharing deals.
- Sharing **governed Genie Agents** is the flashy bet, and also the riskiest: cross-vendor conversational analytics without settled liability is a lawyer's field day.
- **Hype level: Medium.** **Bottom line:** pilot the managed-networking and model/data sharing now; treat cross-vendor agent sharing as a Watch until governance and liability are spelled out.
