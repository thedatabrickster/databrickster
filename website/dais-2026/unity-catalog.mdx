# Unity Catalog — Agentic-Era Governance

> Governance for the agentic era, organized into three pillars — **Control, Context, Choice** — extending Unity Catalog to govern models, agents, and MCP services, not just tables.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/whats-new-unity-catalog-data-ai-summit-2026).*

## Overview

- Unity Catalog is framed as **governance for the agentic era**, structured around three pillars: **Control**, **Context**, and **Choice**.
- **Control:** Unity AI Gateway (GA), Contextual Service Policies (Beta), AI Gateway Budgets / spend caps (GA), Unified Agent Tracing (GA), ABAC (GA; ABAC Grant Policies for models in Beta), and RBAC (Public Preview coming soon).
- **Context:** business Glossary (Preview coming soon), Domains (Public Preview), Metrics (GA), external lineage (GA), and column-level popularity (GA).
- **Choice / Openness:** four-level namespace (GA), managed disaster recovery (GA, requires the Mission Critical add-on), a multimodal `FILE` type (Beta), geospatial types (GA), and OpenSharing (now a Linux Foundation project).
- Governance scope now explicitly covers **models, agents, and MCP services** as first-class governed objects.
- Databricks cites **14,000+ organizations** governing data and AI on Unity Catalog, with **hundreds of thousands of agents** now acting on enterprise data.

## Where it fits

- The **governance and security layer** of the stack: the control plane under agents, Genie, and the AI platform.
- Teams building **agentic workloads** who need one place to set who/what an agent may touch, and to trace what it did.
- **AI Gateway Budgets** and the Unity AI Gateway land where finance and platform teams worry about runaway agent/model costs.
- **ABAC + Contextual Service Policies** fit orgs that have outgrown pure RBAC and need attribute- and context-driven access.
- **Glossary, Domains, Metrics** pair with Genie and BI — shared semantics so natural-language and agent queries mean the same thing.
- **External lineage + column-level popularity** help data platform and governance teams see impact and prune unused assets.
- **Multimodal `FILE` type, geospatial, four-level namespace** suit multi-cloud and unstructured/spatial data shops.

## Where it fails

- **Breadth is complexity.** Three pillars spanning roughly 15 features is a lot of surface area; most orgs will adopt a fraction and still owe migration and training.
- **Mixed maturity.** Core pieces are GA (Unity AI Gateway, Metrics, external lineage, geospatial, four-level namespace), but several land as Beta or Preview (Contextual Service Policies, `FILE` type, RBAC, Glossary) — sequence adoption to real status, not the headline.
- **Four-level namespace migration is not free.** Changing the namespace model ripples through code, grants, and integrations; expect a real project, not a toggle.
- **Governing autonomous agents is genuinely hard.** Policy plus tracing help, but non-deterministic agents acting on live data create audit, blast-radius, and accountability gaps that governance UI does not fully close.
- **Spend caps need honest granularity** — per-agent, per-team, hard vs soft — or AI Gateway Budgets become dashboards, not guardrails.
- **Lock-in tension.** Deep Unity Catalog features cut against open catalogs — **Iceberg REST catalog** and **Apache Polaris / Snowflake Open Catalog** — so weigh portability before committing governance logic here.

## Response

- **Adopt now (core):** if you are already on Unity Catalog, ABAC, Glossary-adjacent metadata, Metrics, and external lineage are the low-risk, high-value GA pieces — keep investing.
- **Pilot:** Unity AI Gateway, agent tracing, and AI Gateway Budgets — valuable and largely GA, but new; run them against one real agentic workload before standardizing.
- **Watch (pre-GA):** Contextual Service Policies (Beta), the `FILE` type (Beta), RBAC and Glossary (Preview) — plan around them but confirm timelines before you depend on them.
- **Market context:** the open-catalog camp (Iceberg REST, Polaris, plus Snowflake Horizon) is the real competitive check — keep table formats interoperable even if you centralize policy in Unity Catalog.

## Personal impression

- The **Control/Context/Choice** framing is a clean story, and extending governance to agents and MCP is the right direction — that is where the risk is moving.
- But "govern the agentic era" is easy to slide and hard to deliver; the honest test is whether spend caps and agent tracing actually constrain a misbehaving agent, not whether they render a nice lineage graph.
- The GA-heavy core (Gateway, Metrics, lineage, geospatial, namespace) is more shipped than not — the agent-policy edges (Contextual Service Policies, RBAC) are where the maturity gap sits.
- **Hype level: Medium.** **Bottom line:** strong, coherent governance vision — adopt the mature GA core, pilot the agent-governance pieces, and keep your table formats open so the catalog stays a choice, not a cage.
