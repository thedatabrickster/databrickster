# AI/BI — Beautiful Dashboards

> Theming and customization for AI/BI — branded, consistent, accessible dashboards across the workspace.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/design-beautiful-dashboards-aibi).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="GA" /> — available now.

## Overview

- Theming and customization for AI/BI dashboards — branded, consistent, and accessible dashboards standardized across the workspace.
- **12-column grid** that subdivides into **3, 4, or 6 columns** — 3-column for executive dashboards to reduce cognitive load, 6-column for dense technical dashboards.
- **Fonts** — web-safe options (Arial, Tahoma, Verdana) plus custom local fonts, with sans-serif recommended to reduce visual strain.
- **Workspace themes** — apply a theme once and it carries across all dashboards in the workspace automatically; individual dashboards also settable via dashboard themes.
- **Light and dark** modes supported by every AI/BI theme, with color guided by the **60-30-10 rule** (60% dominant, 30% secondary, 10% accent).
- **Colorblind-safe palettes** of 5–9 colors, tested against deuteranopia, protanopia, and tritanopia (roughly 4.5% of the global population has some form of color blindness).
- **Layout guidance** — Z-pattern for imagery-heavy dashboards, F-pattern for text/table-heavy dashboards, with larger sizing for the most critical metrics.
- Design polish on top of AI/BI dashboards, not a change to the underlying analytics engine.

## Where it fits

- **Consumption/experience layer** — the presentation skin on AI/BI Dashboards and Genie output, not a new data or modeling capability. See [the overview](/dais-2026).
- Pairs with **workspace themes** so BI teams enforce one brand across many dashboards instead of hand-styling each.
- Benefits **BI/analytics teams and design-conscious orgs** who ship dashboards to executives, customers, or partners where look-and-feel matters.
- The **12-column grid, resizing, and Z/F-pattern** guidance help non-designers lay out something legible without a design review.
- **Accessibility** — colorblind-safe palettes matter for regulated, government, and large-enterprise dashboards with a11y requirements.
- Fits the "**one branded internal reporting surface**" goal — consistent fonts, colors, and light/dark across the workspace.

## Where it fails

- **This is polish and table-stakes, not a moat** — theming does not differentiate a data platform and is trivially matched.
- **Still trails mature BI** — Power BI and Tableau lead on visualization breadth, custom visuals, formatting depth, and interactivity; branded themes don't close that gap.
- **Theming is not analytics depth** — pretty dashboards on shallow models still mislead; design can make a wrong number look authoritative.
- **Low-stakes feature** — nice to have, but rarely the reason anyone picks or leaves a BI tool.
- **Colorblind-safe palettes are a starting point, not a compliance sign-off** — run a real contrast/a11y audit of your own dashboards before claiming compliance.
- **Custom-font and brand rollout** can drift across workspaces without governance, undercutting the "consistent" promise.
- Grid/scanning guidance helps novices but is **no substitute for a real design system** if dashboards are a primary product surface.

## Response

- **Verdict: Adopt now (if you're already on AI/BI).** It's low-risk, no-cost polish — turn on workspace themes and use the colorblind-safe palettes for existing dashboards.
- **Watch** for anyone choosing AI/BI over Power BI or Tableau on the strength of theming — this narrows the aesthetic gap, not the feature gap.
- **Pilot the accessibility palettes** with a real a11y check before claiming compliance.
- **Market context** — closes an obvious cosmetic deficit versus mature BI, but Databricks' actual edge remains governed data plus Genie, not chart styling.

## Personal impression

- Genuinely useful housekeeping — consistent branding, light/dark, and colorblind-safe palettes are things every BI team wants and shouldn't have to hand-build.
- But it's catch-up, not a leap: it makes AI/BI look more professional without making it a Tableau replacement, and theming will never be the reason to bet on the platform.
- **Hype level: Low.** Sensible, welcome, and completely expected table-stakes.
- **Bottom line:** Flip it on if you already use AI/BI; don't let a nicer coat of paint drive the BI-tool decision.
