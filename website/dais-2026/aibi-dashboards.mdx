# AI/BI — Beautiful Dashboards

> The presentation layer AI/BI has been missing — workspace-wide themes, a real grid, and colorblind-safe palettes so dashboards stop looking like prototypes.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/design-beautiful-dashboards-aibi).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="GA" /> — available now.

## What shipped

- **Workspace themes** — set a theme once and it carries across every dashboard in the workspace automatically; individual dashboards can still override via dashboard themes.
- **12-column grid** that subdivides into 3, 4, or 6 columns — 3-column for exec dashboards to cut cognitive load, 6-column for dense technical views.
- **Fonts** — web-safe options (Arial, Tahoma, Verdana) plus custom local fonts, with sans-serif recommended to reduce visual strain.
- **Light and dark** modes on every theme, with color guided by the 60-30-10 rule (60% dominant, 30% secondary, 10% accent).
- **Colorblind-safe palettes** of 5–9 colors, tested against deuteranopia, protanopia, and tritanopia (roughly 4.5% of the global population has some form of color blindness).
- **Layout guidance** — Z-pattern for imagery-heavy dashboards, F-pattern for text/table-heavy ones, with larger sizing for the metrics that matter most.
- Pure design polish on top of AI/BI — the underlying analytics engine is untouched.

## Where it fits

- **Consumption/experience layer** — this is the presentation skin on AI/BI Dashboards and Genie output, not a new data or modeling capability. See [the overview](/dais-2026).
- **Workspace themes** are the real story: BI teams enforce one brand across many dashboards instead of hand-styling each, which is where consistency usually dies.
- Benefits **BI/analytics teams and design-conscious orgs** shipping dashboards to executives, customers, or partners where look-and-feel is part of the credibility.
- The **grid, resizing, and Z/F-pattern** guidance let non-designers lay out something legible without waiting on a design review.
- **Accessibility** — colorblind-safe palettes matter for regulated, government, and large-enterprise dashboards with real a11y requirements.
- Directly serves the "**one branded internal reporting surface**" goal — consistent fonts, colors, and light/dark across the workspace.

## My take

**Adopt now if you're already on AI/BI.** This is zero-cost, low-risk polish: flip on workspace themes, switch existing dashboards to the colorblind-safe palettes, and you've closed an obvious cosmetic gap in an afternoon. There's genuinely nothing to overthink here — consistent branding, light/dark, and accessible palettes are things every BI team wants and shouldn't have to hand-build.

Be honest about what it is, though. This is nice polish, not a moat. AI/BI still trails mature BI — Power BI and Tableau lead on visualization breadth, custom visuals, and formatting depth, and a branded theme doesn't close that gap. Theming is not analytics: a pretty dashboard on a shallow model just makes a wrong number look authoritative. And treat the colorblind-safe palettes as a solid starting point, not a compliance sign-off — run a real contrast/a11y audit before you claim anything.

So turn it on, but don't let a nicer coat of paint drive the BI-tool decision. Databricks' actual edge is still governed data plus Genie, not chart styling — this just stops the dashboards from undercutting that story.

**Bottom line:** Free, sensible, table-stakes polish — enable it today, and keep expectations calibrated to "looks professional," not "replaces Tableau."
