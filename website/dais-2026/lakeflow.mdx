# Lakeflow

> Databricks is trying to make Fivetran + dbt + Airflow one product, on one bill, under one catalog — and with Designer and Zerobus GA, this is further than a roadmap slide.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/lakeflow-new-era-agentic-data-engineering).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="GA" /> — Designer & Zerobus are GA; **Real-Time Mode** is in Public Preview.

## What it is

- One platform for **ingestion, transformation, and orchestration**, governed end-to-end by Unity Catalog — the whole ELT loop instead of three stitched tools.
- **Lakeflow Connect** — 100+ managed connectors. Confluence, HubSpot, and query-based capture for all database connectors are GA; Jira, GitHub, SharePoint, Google Drive, Outlook, and the ad platforms (Meta/TikTok/Google) are Beta. Community Connectors (Beta) cover custom systems.
- **Lakeflow Designer (GA)** — no-code drag-and-drop ETL from natural-language prompts; every visual Flow compiles to a Spark Declarative Pipeline with **"zero translation loss."**
- **Genie Code** — author connectors, build Python/SQL pipelines, and develop jobs from inside Lakeflow.
- **Genie ZeroOps** — a background agent that root-causes issues from quality metrics, logs, and lineage, proposes fixes, and validates them in a sandbox with human-in-the-loop approval.
- **Zerobus (GA)** — near-real-time writes in under 5 seconds, up to 100MB/s (over 10GB/s per table). gRPC/REST and SDKs (Python, Java, Rust, Go, TypeScript) GA; Kafka-compatible APIs Beta; OpenTelemetry Public Preview. **Real-Time Mode** (Public Preview) pushes end-to-end latency as low as 5ms.

## Where it fits

- This is the plumbing layer of the lakehouse — the **ingest + transform + orchestrate** tier everything else sits on.
- Aimed squarely at teams standardizing on Databricks who want to retire a **Fivetran + dbt + Airflow** stitch-up and consolidate under one governance model and one invoice.
- **Connect** suits shops with common SaaS/DB sources and no appetite for maintaining custom connectors; **Designer** targets analysts building pipelines without owning PySpark.
- **Zerobus / Real-Time Mode** covers event-driven and near-real-time work (clickstream, telemetry, operational sync) that previously meant standing up Kafka.
- Pairs naturally with Unity Catalog for lineage and Genie Code for authoring.
- **Proof points:** Panasonic unified SAP, Workday, and SharePoint and cut Power BI refresh times by 50%; Meta drove end-to-end pipeline latency under a minute with Zerobus and Spark Declarative Pipelines.

## Where it falls short

- **"Zero translation loss" is the load-bearing claim.** Visual-to-code compilers have always leaked on custom logic, edge cases, and performance tuning. Take it as a hypothesis to disprove on your own pipelines, not a guarantee.
- **No-code ETL has a ceiling.** Fine for the simple 80%; complex joins, incremental CDC, testing, and CI/CD still push serious teams back to code. Designer lowers the floor, it doesn't raise the roof.
- **Agentic auto-fix is high blast radius.** A bot that root-causes and patches production pipelines is genuinely useful and genuinely dangerous. Sandbox validation and human-in-the-loop help, but approval gates and an audit trail are non-negotiable — the trust curve, not the tech, decides whether teams turn it on.
- **A lot of the surface is Beta/Preview.** Kafka-compatible APIs, most knowledge/MarTech connectors, and Real-Time Mode are still pre-GA — production readiness varies by component.
- **Consolidation is lock-in.** Folding Fivetran/dbt/Airflow into Lakeflow deepens Databricks dependence and removes portability, and a 100+ connector catalog rarely matches Fivetran's long tail on every source.

## My take

This is the most strategically coherent piece of the stack. Owning ingest-to-orchestration under one governance model is exactly where Databricks should be pushing, and unlike a lot of Summit announcements, the important parts ship GA today — Connect, Designer, and Zerobus are real, not roadmap. The numbers (under-5s writes, over 10GB/s per table, 5ms Real-Time Mode) are now Databricks' own, so hold them to it.

**Adopt where GA, pilot the Preview surface.** Run Connect and Designer on a non-critical pipeline first and confirm "zero translation loss" and connector coverage actually hold for *your* sources — noting which connectors are still Beta. Zerobus is credible for near-real-time writes; validate the throughput against your own workload. Only trial Real-Time Mode if you have a genuine sub-second need.

For Genie ZeroOps, enable suggest-only mode and never let it auto-apply to prod. The upside is real; so is the failure mode. And keep an exit path — the incumbents you're consolidating are deeper and portable, and consolidation economics cut both ways.

**Bottom line:** a strong consolidation story and a credible shot at Fivetran/dbt/Airflow — adopt the GA pieces, pilot the Previews, and don't let a bot patch prod unattended.
