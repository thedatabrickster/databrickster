# AI Platform — Real-Time ML

> Agents for ML engineering, serverless GPU training, and high-QPS real-time serving — a "raw data to production" ML story on one Unity Catalog governance model.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/whats-new-ai-platform-agents-ml-engineering-our-deep-learning-platform-and-new-capabilities).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="GA" /> — Model Serving & Genie Code for ML are GA; **AI Runtime** is in Public Preview.

## Overview

- Three pieces: **Genie Code for ML Engineering** (**GA**), the **serverless AI Runtime** deep-learning platform (**Public Preview**), and enhanced **real-time Model Serving** (**GA**).
- **Genie Code for ML Engineering** is Unity Catalog-aware and integrates natively with Feature Store, Serverless Compute, AI Runtime, Model Serving, and Inference Tables — covering feature engineering, training/tuning, and deployment to production endpoints, with MLflow across the full lifecycle.
- **AI Runtime** attaches **serverless A10 and H100 GPUs in 2–3 clicks**, with **pay-per-use** (no idle time or upfront commitment), **multinode training**, **RDMA**, and optimized data loading; orchestrated via Lakeflow Jobs and DABs.
- **High-QPS Model Serving** reaches **300K+ QPS at under 10ms p99 latency overhead**, handles **100K+ QPS in production**, supports CPU and GPU models, and delivers **up to 2x** better p99 and p50 latency with infrastructure costs reduced **up to 90%+** versus self-managed alternatives.
- **Feature Store** adds declarative feature engineering (define once, materialize for training and serving), **Streaming Features**, and online serving on **Lakebase**.
- **Genie ZeroOps for ML** brings agentic observability — inference-table querying, endpoint debugging, and alert root-cause analysis.

## Where it fits

- **Classic ML teams on Databricks** who want feature store, training, and serving under one Unity Catalog governance boundary.
- Genie Code targets the **glue work** — feature pipelines, training scaffolds, deployment configs, monitoring hooks — where MLOps teams lose the most time.
- Serverless A10/H100 GPUs suit **bursty training and fine-tuning** without standing up and babysitting a cluster.
- **Real-time serving** fits fraud, recommendations, pricing, and personalization where features must be fresh and latency is user-facing.
- Pairs with **Lakeflow** (ingestion/orchestration), **Lakebase** (online features), and **Genie ZeroOps** (the alert root-cause layer).
- Attractive if you already run MLflow + feature engineering on Databricks and want to avoid a separate serving stack.

## Where it fails

- **GPU availability and cost are the real constraint.** "2–3 clicks to an H100" means nothing if capacity is queued — validate actual availability and pay-per-use rates for your region.
- **Headline QPS is overhead, not a promise.** 300K+ QPS at under 10ms p99 *latency overhead* is the platform's number; your end-to-end p99 depends on model, payload size, and feature-fetch cost.
- The **up to 2x** latency and **up to 90%+** cost figures are best-case ceilings — "up to" leaves the typical case unstated.
- **Classic ML vs GenAI focus is muddy.** Feature stores and real-time serving are classic-ML concerns; confirm the pieces you need aren't GenAI-first.
- **Mature competitors already do this**: SageMaker (feature store, real-time endpoints, multinode training) and Vertex AI (feature store, online serving) are GA and battle-tested.
- **Preview maturity risk on AI Runtime** — it is Public Preview; expect rough edges before GA.
- **Lock-in**: UC-aware convenience only pays off if your features, models, and serving all live in Databricks; it doesn't transfer.

## Response

- **Verdict: Pilot** — if you're already on Databricks for ML and feel the feature-store-to-serving pain, a scoped real-time use case is worth testing.
- **Adopt Genie Code** cautiously — it's GA, but a coding agent spanning feature-eng through monitoring is a wide surface; start on low-risk workflows and expand.
- **Ignore** if you're happy on SageMaker/Vertex with a working feature store and endpoints — there's no forced-migration case here.
- Benchmark **your** real-time serving: end-to-end p99 under production QPS, feature freshness, and cost per million predictions — not the platform's overhead number.
- Market context: this is Databricks answering **SageMaker and Vertex** on real-time + feature stores; the wedge is **one Unity Catalog governance model**.

## Personal impression

- Strategically sound — unifying feature engineering, training, serving, and monitoring under Unity Catalog with an agent doing the glue is exactly where MLOps teams bleed time.
- This is the most crowded, most mature category Databricks competes in, but the serving numbers (300K+ QPS, under 10ms p99 overhead, up to 2x latency) are now concrete rather than hand-wavy.
- The GPU-in-a-few-clicks pitch still lives or dies on **actual capacity and price**, and AI Runtime is only Public Preview.
- **Hype level: Medium.**
- **Bottom line:** credible, now-GA serving and agent story for Databricks-committed ML shops; pilot one real-time workload against your own p99 and cost, and don't migrate off a working SageMaker/Vertex stack reflexively.
