# Lakehouse//RT

> A real-time data warehouse that queries the lakehouse in milliseconds — no separate serving store, no data duplication.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/introducing-lakehousert-real-time-performance-unified-lakehouse).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="Beta" /> — select read-only workloads.

## Overview

- A **real-time data warehouse** powered by a new **"Reyden" engine**, built for high-concurrency, low-latency serving.
- Serves **Delta and Iceberg directly under Unity Catalog** — no duplicate pipelines, no separate serving layer, no copied policies.
- **Up to 16x better performance** than real-time serving layers; **10ms on smaller datasets**, **sub-100ms on larger ones**; **sub-100ms latency at 12,000 queries/second**, with flat latency across thousands of concurrent queries.
- **AUTO sizing** replaces manual t-shirt sizing; **incremental autoscaling** adds/removes single nodes rather than whole warehouse copies.
- **Status: Beta**, available for select read-only workloads, with more capabilities coming.
- **30% introductory discount through January 2027**; usage-based billing.
- Preview customers include **Meta, SES, Enverus, Magnite, Cisco, Halcyon AI, PointClickCare, Bally's, and Equilibrium Energy**.

## Where it fits

- **Real-time OLAP on the lakehouse** — dashboards, operational analytics, and user-facing metrics without shipping data to a separate store.
- Best for teams **already standardized on Unity Catalog** with Delta/Iceberg who want one governance model across batch and real-time.
- Removes a common failure point: the **ETL-to-serving-layer hop** and its duplicated pipelines, policies, and drift.
- Pairs naturally with **Lakebase/LTAP** (transactional), **Lakeflow** (ingestion), and AI/BI dashboards for the last-mile serving.
- Attractive where queries are **relationally complex** (multi-table joins, windows) — the historical weak spot of Druid/Pinot-style engines.
- The AUTO sizing / single-node autoscaling story targets **cost efficiency** for spiky, unpredictable real-time load.

## Where it fails

- **Beta, read-only.** It's limited to select read-only workloads today; write paths and broader capabilities are still coming.
- **Benchmarks are vendor-framed.** "Up to 16x" and "12,000 QPS at sub-100ms" need independent, workload-matched validation against your own queries.
- Headline latencies (10ms small data, sub-100ms at scale) are **best-case**; real-world tail latency under concurrency and complex joins is what matters and isn't shown.
- Purpose-built engines — **ClickHouse, Druid, Pinot, Rockset** — remain extremely strong on high-QPS point/aggregation workloads and may still win on raw latency and cost.
- **Lock-in risk**: the value depends on Unity Catalog + Databricks; the "no duplication" benefit doesn't transfer if your serving lives elsewhere.
- Named preview customers are **marketing signals, not benchmarks** — different data, queries, and baselines.
- The 30% intro discount through Jan 2027 reflects **early-stage pricing**; watch for cost changes after the promo.

## Response

- **Verdict: Pilot** — if you already run Delta/Iceberg on Unity Catalog and have a concrete real-time serving pain, the "no separate layer" pitch is worth a scoped test while it's in Beta.
- **Watch** if you're happy with an existing OLAP engine — don't rip out a working ClickHouse/Druid/Pinot deployment on a Beta.
- Benchmark against your **actual** queries and QPS, including complex joins and p99 latency under concurrency — not the demo numbers.
- Market context: this competes with real-time OLAP (**ClickHouse, Druid, Pinot, Rockset**) and warehouse serving (Snowflake, BigQuery); the wedge is **governance unification and no data movement**, not necessarily raw speed.

## Personal impression

- The strategic idea is right: killing the copy-to-serving-layer step and keeping one Unity Catalog governance boundary is genuinely valuable if the latency holds under real load.
- The numbers are strong on paper, but complex-join performance at high QPS is exactly where these claims usually soften — and it's still Beta and read-only.
- **Hype level: High.**
- **Bottom line:** compelling direction for lakehouse-committed shops; pilot it against your own workload before believing a single benchmark, and account for its Beta, read-only scope.
