# Lakebase & LTAP

> Postgres inside the lakehouse is real and shipping today. The "one copy serves both OLTP and OLAP" story — LTAP — is still a roadmap slide.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Sources: [Databricks LTAP press release](https://www.databricks.com/company/newsroom/press-releases/databricks-launches-ltap-first-lake-transactionalanalytical) · [Lakebase launch partners](https://www.databricks.com/blog/announcing-databricks-lakebase-launch-partners).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="GA" /> — Lakebase is GA; the new **LTAP** architecture is *coming soon*.

## What shipped
- **Lakebase is GA**: fully managed, serverless PostgreSQL wired into the Data Intelligence Platform. This is the part you can actually build on today.
- **LTAP** (Lake Transactional/Analytical Processing) is the *architecture* — OLTP and OLAP on one governed storage layer, marketed as a "world's first." It is **coming soon**, not GA. Treat it as direction, not a feature you can buy.
- Premise: **one governed copy** — Lakebase and the lakehouse share open formats (**Delta and Iceberg**), so operational data is queryable for analytics without a separate copy.
- Real operational muscle: **cross-cloud, cross-region DR**; **git-style branching and snapshots** against production data; **autonomous ops** (health monitoring, slowdown detection, index proposals, recovery assistance).
- **Unity Catalog** governance — one identity and permission model spanning transactional and analytical data. This, not the engine, is the actual differentiator.
- Scale claims: **12M database launches/day**, **20,000+ organizations**, **70% of the Fortune 500**. Named customers: **Block, Ensemble, Superhuman, Zillow**. **60+ launch partners** (Accenture, Deloitte, EY, Fivetran, Confluent, Retool, Replit, Thoughtspot).

## Where it fits
- **The operational layer the lakehouse never had** — a first-class transactional store instead of bolting on an external database.
- **Agent backing store** — state, memory, and config for agents. This is the partner-cited primary use, and it's a genuinely good fit.
- **Greenfield app backends** that want Postgres semantics without standing up a separate managed database.
- Teams that want **fresh operational data in analytics** without CDC pipelines or nightly loads.
- Standardizing on the **Postgres wire protocol** means existing drivers and tooling mostly just work.
- **Branching/snapshots** are a real win for dev/test, blue-green data changes, and reproducible experiments.

## Where it falls short
- **OLTP on object storage fights physics.** Object stores have high per-op latency; low-latency, high-concurrency writes need a caching/local tier. Ask for real transaction latency numbers, not batch throughput.
- **"Single copy" and workload isolation are in tension.** True isolation usually means separate storage/compute paths — expect nuance behind the one-copy headline.
- **Postgres compatibility gaps** are likely early: extensions, edge SQL features, specific isolation-level behavior. Test your actual workload.
- **LTAP is not GA.** The whole unified transactional/analytical pitch is "coming soon." Today you get Lakebase; plan around that, not the roadmap.
- **"World's first" is marketing.** HTAP is a decade-old category and Snowflake Unistore exists. The claim is about the governance framing, not a new invention.
- **Lock-in.** A Databricks-native Postgres deepens platform dependence versus a portable managed Postgres you can lift out.

## My take
The useful thing here ships now: **governed Postgres inside the lakehouse**, with a natural home as agent memory and config. That solves a real gap — you stop running a separate database and stitching it to Unity Catalog by hand. For agent state and net-new app backends, **pilot it**; the convenience is real and the blast radius is small.

LTAP is the ambitious bet, and I'd **watch, not adopt**. Unifying OLTP and OLAP on one copy is genuinely attractive, but object-storage OLTP has known physics working against it, and it's pre-GA with no independent latency, concurrency, or isolation numbers. Don't move a system of record onto it on the strength of a launch keynote. The boring alternative — **managed Postgres/Aurora + warehouse + CDC** — is portable, battle-tested, and known-cost. Also model serverless pricing on hot transactional paths before committing; it can surprise you.

**Bottom line:** adopt Lakebase now for agent state and greenfield apps; keep systems of record on proven Postgres/Aurora until LTAP is GA and survives independent benchmarks.
