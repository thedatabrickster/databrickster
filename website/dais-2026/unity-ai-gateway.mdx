# Unity AI Gateway

> Govern runtime AI — models, agents, MCP services, and skills — through a single access, cost, and audit layer.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/ai-governance-data-ai-summit-2026-whats-new-unity-ai-gateway).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="GA" label="Core" /> <StatusBadge stage="Beta" label="Contextual policies, MCP gov" />

## Overview

- Control plane for **runtime AI traffic**: Databricks-hosted models, external/frontier models, agents, MCP services, and skills sit behind one governed layer.
- **Cost governance** — unified spend visibility across hosted models, frontier model families, coding agents, enterprise AI apps, and custom agents, with attribution by user, team, tool, and use case, plus **hard spend caps that automatically stop requests when budgets are exceeded**.
- **Smart routing** — recommendations and request routing based on task complexity, quality requirements, and cost.
- **Fine-grained policies** — Unity Catalog access policies applied dynamically by model provider, country of origin, approval status, or governed tags; plus **managed MCP services** (Google Drive, Jira, Confluence, Slack, GitHub, SharePoint) and custom MCP.
- **Guardrails** — protection against PII exposure, prompt injection, jailbreaks, and unsafe content.
- **Observability** — end-to-end agent traces (model interactions + MCP tool activity), Genie natural-language log exploration, and Lakewatch-based detection of suspicious activity and policy violations.
- **Status** — core cost controls, spend visibility, hard caps, and smart routing are **GA**. Contextual Service Policies, Managed Omnigent on Databricks, and MCP service governance are **Beta**.

## Where it fits

- **Governance & security layer** of the Modern Databricks Stack — the runtime-AI counterpart to Unity Catalog's data governance. See [the overview](/dais-2026).
- Central chokepoint for **FinOps on AI**: attribute spend to users, teams, tools, and use cases, and set caps before the bill surprises you.
- Fits orgs running **multiple model providers** (foundation, hosted, external) who want one policy surface instead of per-provider consoles.
- Pairs naturally with **Agent Bricks, Genie, and MCP services** — anything that emits or consumes model calls at runtime.
- Compliance value: **country-of-origin, provider, and approval-status policies** help with data-residency and approved-vendor mandates.
- Useful where **audit trails** for agent behavior are required — end-to-end traces feed Lakewatch and Genie for investigation.

## Where it fails

- **Chokepoint and latency** — routing every model call through a gateway adds a hop; real-time/low-latency agent loops may feel it. No published latency figures.
- **Only governs traffic that flows through it** — direct SDK calls, notebook-embedded keys, or third-party tools bypass it. **Shadow AI remains the hole in the fence.**
- **MCP governance is Beta** — MCP service governance and Contextual Service Policies are early; expect coverage gaps versus the GA cost-control surface.
- **Routing quality is a judgment call** — "smart routing" ranks on task complexity, quality, and cost, but how quality is scored and how reliably is not detailed. Validate on your own workloads.
- **Lock-in** — centralizing routing, policy, and attribution inside Databricks makes it costly to move governance out later.
- Overlaps with existing tooling (below) — teams already standardized on an AI gateway will weigh migration cost against marginal Unity integration.

## Response

- **Verdict: Adopt for cost control, pilot the rest.** Unified spend visibility, attribution, and hard caps are GA and the clearest near-term win.
- **Adopt now if** you are already on Unity Catalog and want one governance surface — the native lineage/policy integration is the differentiator, not the gateway mechanics.
- **Pilot** the Beta pieces (Contextual Service Policies, MCP service governance, Managed Omnigent) until they mature.
- **Market context** — competes with **LiteLLM** (open-source proxy), **Kong AI Gateway**, **Portkey**, and cloud-native gateways (Bedrock, Azure AI). Databricks' edge is native Unity lineage/policy.
- Don't rip out a working gateway for this alone; the value is Unity integration, so scope adoption to that.

## Personal impression

- The genuinely useful idea is **treating AI spend and access like any other governed resource** — attribution and hard caps solve a real, painful problem, and they shipped GA.
- A gateway only governs what passes through it, and "smart routing" on quality is exactly the claim to validate. Shadow AI and self-served endpoints will leak around it.
- **Hype level: Medium.** Strong FinOps hook with GA cost controls; MCP governance and contextual policies are still Beta.
- **Bottom line:** Adopt for cost control and attribution today; pilot the routing intelligence and MCP-governance pieces as they exit Beta.
