# Unity AI Gateway

> Govern runtime AI — models, agents, MCP services, and skills — through a single access, cost, and audit layer.

*Announced at DAIS 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: community deck — verify officially.*

## Overview

- Positioned as the control plane for **runtime AI traffic**: models, agents, MCP services, and skills all sit behind one governed layer.
- **Cost governance** — unified AI spend with granular attribution (by user, team, project, tag) plus **hard spend caps**.
- **Smart routing** — quality/cost-aware routing across providers and models, per the deck.
- **Fine-grained policies** — enforce by provider, country, approval status, or tag; plus **managed MCP services**.
- **Observability** — end-to-end agent tracing, Genie log exploration, and LakeWatch-based detection.
- Deck metrics (unverified): governs models + agents + MCP + skills in one layer; **auto-stop on budget breach**; AI-security/identity partner integrations; contextual per-service policies.

## Where it fits

- **Governance & security layer** of the Modern Databricks Stack — the runtime-AI counterpart to Unity Catalog's data governance. See [the overview](/dais-2026).
- Central chokepoint for **FinOps on AI**: attribute LLM spend to cost centers, set caps before the bill surprises you.
- Fits orgs running **multiple model providers** (foundation models, hosted, self-served) who want one policy surface instead of per-provider consoles.
- Pairs naturally with **Agent Bricks, Genie, and MCP services** — anything that emits or consumes model calls at runtime.
- Compliance value: **country/provider policies** help with data-residency and approved-vendor mandates.
- Useful where **audit trails** for agent behavior are required (regulated industries, internal risk review).

## Where it fails

- **Chokepoint and latency** — routing every model call through a gateway adds a hop; real-time/low-latency agent loops may feel it. No published latency figures.
- **Only governs traffic that flows through it** — direct SDK calls, notebook-embedded keys, or third-party tools bypass it. **Shadow AI remains the hole in the fence.**
- **MCP governance maturity** — managed MCP services and skill governance are new; expect preview-grade coverage and gaps versus the marketing surface.
- **Routing-quality claims are unverified** — "smart quality/cost routing" implies the gateway judges output quality; how, and how reliably, is unstated. Treat as a claim, not a feature.
- **Metrics unverified** — auto-stop-on-budget, partner integrations, and contextual policies are deck-attributed and not confirmed against official docs.
- **Lock-in** — centralizing routing, policy, and attribution inside Databricks makes it costly to move governance out later.
- Overlaps with existing tooling (below) — teams already standardized on an AI gateway will weigh migration cost against marginal Unity integration.

## Response

- **Verdict: Pilot.** The cost-attribution and spend-cap story is the clearest near-term win; validate it on real spend before betting governance on it.
- **Adopt now only if** you are already all-in on Unity Catalog and want one governance surface — the integration is the differentiator, not the gateway mechanics.
- **Watch** the MCP/skills and routing-quality pieces until previews mature and official docs confirm the deck's claims.
- **Market context** — competes with **LiteLLM** (open-source proxy), **Kong AI Gateway**, **Portkey**, and cloud-native gateways (Bedrock, Azure AI). Databricks' edge is native Unity lineage/policy, not raw gateway features.
- Don't rip out a working gateway for this alone; the value is Unity integration, so scope the pilot to that.

## Personal impression

- The genuinely useful idea here is **treating AI spend and access like any other governed resource** — attribution and hard caps solve a real, painful problem.
- But a gateway only governs what passes through it, and "smart routing" plus "quality-aware" routing are exactly the claims vendors overstate. Shadow AI and self-served endpoints will leak around it.
- **Hype level: Medium.** Strong FinOps hook, thin verified detail on routing and MCP governance.
- **Bottom line:** Pilot for cost control and attribution; stay skeptical of the routing-intelligence and MCP-governance promises until Databricks publishes specifics.
