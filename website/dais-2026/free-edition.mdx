# Free Edition

> Free Edition now includes Genie Code, Serverless GPUs, Lakebase, Agent Bricks, and Lakeflow Designer — a genuinely broad free tier, and a smart adoption flywheel.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/whats-coming-next-free-edition).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="GA" /> — available now.

## What it is

- Five products added to Free Edition so a learner can go **end-to-end** — data engineering, ML training, analysis, and agent building — at no cost.
- The additions: **Genie Code, Serverless GPUs, Lakebase, Agent Bricks, and Lakeflow Designer**.
- Databricks cites **500,000+ Free Edition users** and **over $10M in free credits** granted to learners; hackathon projects range from grid-failure prediction to NLP recommenders.

## Where it fits

- The **on-ramp** of the stack: the try-before-you-buy surface below the paid workspace.
- Best for **skill-building and portfolios** — students and career-switchers can run real projects without a cloud bill. **Serverless GPUs on a free tier is the standout**; touching training/fine-tuning normally means paid infra.
- Doubles as a **cheap evaluation sandbox** for teams sizing up Databricks before committing.

## My take

I don't have much to criticize here, and I think that's the point. Giving away real tooling — including free GPUs — builds a generation of practitioners who reach for Databricks by default, and lets hiring managers do the conversion later. It's self-interested in the best way: the learner wins *and* the vendor wins.

The only thing to keep honest is the word "complete." Free means quotas — capped compute, GPU minutes, storage, region, and community-only support — and it is explicitly **not a production platform**. So read the limits before you build a course or team-onboarding on it, and never ship real workloads from it.

**Bottom line:** an easy yes for anyone learning or evaluating — arguably the most practitioner-friendly free tier in the market — as long as you treat it as a sandbox, not a runtime.
