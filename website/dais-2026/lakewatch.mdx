# Lakewatch — Agentic SIEM

> A SIEM rebuilt on the lakehouse: keep 100% of your telemetry in your own OCSF buckets, decouple storage from compute to kill the retention bill, and hunt in natural language via Genie. The economics are the wedge — detection content is the open question.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/databricks-announces-lakewatch-new-agentic-siem).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="Private Preview" />

## What it is
- Databricks' agentic SIEM on the lakehouse, in **Private Preview** with **Adobe** and **Dropbox** among initial customers.
- Ingests and retains **100% of security telemetry** on the open **OCSF** schema, stored as **Delta Lake / Apache Iceberg** in your own cloud storage — not a vendor index.
- **Decouples compute from storage** so you keep petabytes for years at a claimed **up to 80% lower cost** than traditional SIEMs.
- **Genie** and **Genie Spaces** let analysts threat-hunt in natural language, no SPL or KQL required.
- Detection is **Detection-as-Code** (YAML + SQL/Python), custom **ML models via MLflow**, and **Claude** (Anthropic) driving threat correlation.
- Backed by two acquisitions: **Antimatter** (provable authN/authZ for AI agents) and **SiftD.ai** (founded by the creator of Splunk's SPL; large-scale detection engineering).

## Where it fits
- The **security analytics / detection-and-response layer** on top of Unity Catalog governance, feeding on telemetry already landing in the lakehouse.
- **SOC and threat-hunting teams** who want ad-hoc hunts over huge log volumes without SPL/KQL fluency — OCSF normalization plus Genie lowers the barrier.
- **Cost-pressured security orgs** bleeding on Splunk/Sentinel ingest and retention; decoupled storage/compute is the whole economic pitch.
- **Cross-domain investigations** — co-locating security telemetry with other governed data enables insider-risk and fraud correlation that siloed SIEMs handle poorly.
- **Regulated / data-residency shops** that need telemetry to stay in their own buckets on open formats.
- Pairs naturally with **Genie**, **Unity Catalog**, **MLflow**, and **Claude**.

## Where it falls short
- **SIEM is deeply entrenched.** Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel, and Google Chronicle/SecOps own the market on mature detection content and SOC integrations — this is a contested category, not a greenfield.
- **Detection content is the real moat, and it's the hardest thing to bootstrap.** Incumbents ship thousands of tuned rules, ATT&CK mappings, and threat-intel feeds; a new platform starts near zero. The **SiftD.ai** acquisition is a direct, honest bet on closing that gap — but a bet, not a shipped library.
- **Agentic detection cuts both ways.** LLM-assisted rules and hunting risk **false negatives** (missed attacks) and noisy **false positives** — and a confident-but-wrong rule is worse than no rule in a SOC.
- **The urgency metrics lack per-customer methodology.** The mean-time-to-exploit collapse (23.2 days in 2025 to 1.6 days in 2026) and "500+ zero days found by LLMs" make the case for speed but aren't your benchmarks.
- **Tool inertia in security is brutal.** SOCs are wired to existing consoles, runbooks, SOAR playbooks, and analyst muscle memory. Rip-and-replace is rare and slow, and interactive triage latency over petabytes vs a purpose-built index is unproven.

## My take
The strategic read is correct: SIEM economics are broken, OCSF is the right open bet, and keeping 100% of telemetry in your own storage at up-to-80% lower cost is a wedge incumbents genuinely can't match without cannibalizing their ingest revenue. That part I believe.

What Databricks clearly understands — and the **Antimatter** and **SiftD.ai** acquisitions prove it — is that the hard part was never storage. It's detection engineering and agent security. Buying the creator of Splunk's SPL is a pointed, self-aware move. But acquisitions are potential, not tuned rules in production, and detection maturity is exactly what a Private Preview can't yet demonstrate.

So: **pilot for cost, watch for detection.** Stand Lakewatch up as a security data lake — the warm/cold retention and threat-hunting tier — behind your existing SIEM. Offload the petabyte retention bill, run Genie hunts against it, and keep Splunk/Sentinel/Chronicle doing detection until Lakewatch's content and your SOC's trust catch up. Do not rip anything out yet; years of accumulated rules and workflow don't transfer on a slide.

**Bottom line:** A credible, well-architected security data lake with the right open bet and the right acquisitions — pilot it for retention economics now, but let detection-content maturity and SOC trust earn the displacement of an incumbent.
