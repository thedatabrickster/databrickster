# Lakewatch — Agentic SIEM

> An agentic SIEM rebuilt on the lakehouse: 100% security telemetry in your own cloud storage on open OCSF, storage/compute decoupled, and threat hunting in natural language via Genie — positioned against traditional SIEMs.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/databricks-announces-lakewatch-new-agentic-siem).*

## Overview
- **Lakewatch** is Databricks' agentic SIEM built on the lakehouse, launched in **Private Preview** with **Adobe** and **Dropbox** among initial customers.
- Ingests and retains **100% of security telemetry** on the **open OCSF schema** (Open Cybersecurity Schema Framework), stored as **Delta Lake / Apache Iceberg** in your own cloud storage.
- **Decoupling compute from storage** lets you keep petabytes for years while **reducing costs by up to 80%** versus traditional SIEMs.
- **Genie**-powered natural-language queries and **Genie Spaces** let analysts threat-hunt without writing code.
- Detection is **Detection-as-Code** in YAML with **SQL / Python**, plus custom **ML models via MLflow**; **Claude** (Anthropic) powers threat correlation.
- Two acquisitions announced: **Antimatter** (provably secure authN/authZ for AI agents) and **SiftD.ai** (founded by the creator of Splunk's SPL; large-scale detection engineering).

## Where it fits
- **Security analytics / detection-and-response layer** — sits on Unity Catalog governance, drawing telemetry already landing in the lakehouse.
- **SOC and threat-hunting teams**: OCSF normalization plus Genie natural-language querying lowers the barrier to ad-hoc hunts over huge log volumes.
- **Cost-pressured security orgs** drowning in Splunk/Sentinel ingest and retention bills — decoupled storage/compute driving up to 80% savings is the core economic pitch.
- **Cross-domain investigations**: co-locating security telemetry with other governed data enables insider-risk and fraud correlation classic SIEMs handle poorly.
- **Regulated / data-residency shops**: telemetry stays in your own cloud buckets (Delta / Iceberg), not a vendor's index.
- Pairs with **Genie**, **Genie Spaces**, **Unity Catalog**, **MLflow**, and **Claude** for cross-domain reasoning.

## Where it fails
- **SIEM is deeply entrenched**: Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel, Google Chronicle/SecOps, and Panther own the market with mature detection content and SOC integrations.
- **Detection-content maturity is the moat, and it's the hardest to bootstrap** — incumbents ship thousands of tuned rules, ATT&CK mappings, and threat-intel feeds; a new platform starts near zero (the SiftD.ai acquisition is a direct bet on closing this gap).
- **Agentic detection cuts both ways**: LLM-assisted hunting and rules risk **false negatives** (missed attacks) and noisy **false positives**.
- **Metrics need context**: the mean-time-to-exploit collapse (23.2 days in 2025 to 1.6 days in 2026) and "500+ zero days found by LLMs" argue urgency but come without per-customer methodology.
- **Tool inertia is brutal in security**: SOCs are built around existing consoles, runbooks, SOAR wiring, and analyst muscle memory — rip-and-replace is rare and slow.
- **Full lakehouse query latency vs a purpose-built SIEM index** is unproven for interactive, real-time triage at petabyte scale.
- **Private Preview**: limited availability, small initial customer set, and evolving detection library mean production readiness is still ahead.

## Response
- **Verdict: Watch — with a narrow Pilot for cost.** The economics of decoupled storage/compute on OCSF are genuinely compelling; detection maturity is what to prove out.
- **Pilot as a security data lake / cold-and-warm tier** behind your existing SIEM — offload retention and threat-hunting, keep incumbent detection running.
- **Do not rip out Splunk/Sentinel/Chronicle** yet; detection content and SOC workflow are years of accumulated value, and Lakewatch is in Private Preview.
- **Track the ecosystem**: partners include Akamai, Anvilogic, Arctic Wolf, Cribl, Deloitte, Obsidian, Okta, 1Password, Palo Alto Networks, Panther, Proofpoint, Rearc, Slack, TrendAI, Wiz, and Zscaler.
- Market context: the "security data lake" pattern (Snowflake + Panther, Amazon Security Lake on OCSF, Cribl routing) already exists — Databricks is entering a real, contested category, not inventing it.

## Personal impression
- The strategic logic is sound — SIEM economics are broken, OCSF is the right open bet, and up-to-80% cost cuts on your own storage is a real wedge incumbents can't easily match.
- The Antimatter and SiftD.ai acquisitions show Databricks knows the hard part is detection engineering and agent security, not storage — buying the creator of Splunk's SPL is a pointed move.
- Agentic detection is the seductive claim and the riskiest; in security a confident-but-wrong LLM rule is worse than no rule.
- **Hype level: High.** **Bottom line:** A credible, officially-launched security-data-lake and threat-hunting platform worth piloting alongside your SIEM — but it's Private Preview, and detection-content maturity and SOC trust are what to validate before it displaces an incumbent.
