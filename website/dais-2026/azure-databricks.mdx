# Azure Databricks at DAIS 2026

> The real headline here isn't a feature — it's Databricks planting a flag as the default lakehouse on Azure, and backing it with governance plumbing (OneLake) and customer logos rather than net-new tech.

*Announced at Data + AI Summit 2026 (June 16–17, 2026). Source: [Databricks blog](https://www.databricks.com/blog/azure-databricks-data-ai-summit-2026-featuring-industry-leaders-and-partners).*

**Stage:** <StatusBadge stage="Coming soon" /> — OneLake external locations are coming soon; the ADME solution is available.

## What shipped
- **Unity Catalog external locations for OneLake** (coming soon) — extends UC governance to Microsoft OneLake storage, joining existing S3, ADLS, GCS, and Cloudflare R2 support. UC then governs everything built on top (managed tables, views, other assets) with no ETL pipeline to stand up.
- **Zero-copy federated analytics for Azure Data Manager for Energy (ADME)** — a jointly built Microsoft-Databricks solution that runs analytics on ADME data in place. Databricks compute comes to the data with native governance and serverless scale; ADME stays the single source of truth, no massive downstream copies.
- **GEODIS** — modernized its data/AI platform on Azure Databricks using Apache Iceberg and Spark.
- **TK Elevator** — consolidated a fragmented data lake, wiring management reporting to field operations.
- **Lippert** — production-grade AI automation for finance workflows on Azure Document Intelligence, delivered with partner Lovelytics.
- No performance benchmarks were published.

## Where it fits
- **Governance + integration layer** for shops already standardized on Azure and Microsoft Fabric.
- Teams with data landing in **OneLake** who want UC governance over it without a second copy.
- **Energy** teams on ADME wanting analytics over operational/subsurface data in place, no downstream duplication.
- **Logistics and manufacturing** modernizers (GEODIS, TK Elevator) collapsing siloed lakes.
- **Finance/FP&A** teams (Lippert) automating document-heavy workflows with governed AI.
- Pairs with **Unity Catalog** as the single governance plane, **Delta/Iceberg** interop, and existing **Microsoft 365 / Power BI** estates.
- Best fit where the buyer wants **one Azure-native stack** over stitching multiple analytics vendors.

## My take
If you're Azure-committed, this is a genuinely useful update — but read it for what it is: a go-to-market package, not a platform release. The technology underneath (Unity Catalog, Delta/Iceberg, serverless) already existed. What's new is the wrapping — OneLake reach, an energy vertical solution, and three logos to point at.

The **OneLake external-location** work is the one to actually track, and it's also where I'd hold the line. It's *coming soon*, not GA, so there's no date, and the OneLake tie deepens your dependence on Microsoft Fabric — which Microsoft simultaneously sells *against* Databricks. Partner and competitor in the same breath. Validate write paths, permission mapping, and performance yourself before you plan production around it, and evaluate Fabric's native lakehouse head-to-head rather than taking the Databricks framing at face value.

Everything else nets out cleanly: **adopt** the Unity Catalog governance story now — it's stable and pays off independent of the Azure packaging. **Pilot** ADME zero-copy federation only if you're already an energy customer on that stack; otherwise it's noise. And treat GEODIS/TK Elevator/Lippert as reference calls to *request* — the outcomes ship without independent cost, timeline, or migration-effort data, so mine peers for the real numbers.

**Bottom line:** solid if you're Azure-first, but the headline OneLake integration is still coming soon and the Fabric relationship cuts both ways — bank the Unity Catalog governance, watch the rest.
